The Trail Guide for Lazy Hikers
Glacier National Park
About Us!

Our mission is to provide a platform for hikers to share their experiences and help others find the best trails. Our trails have been reviewed by our community of hikers and are rated based on difficulty, length, and scenery. We always do our best to provide a well rounded review of each trail, so you can make an informed decision on which trail to hike next. We hope you enjoy our site and find it helpful in planning your next hike!

Baring Falls

An easily accessible trail that takes you along the river and near Baring falls! This trail is very safe for family and friends while still holding the beauty our park holds.

St. Mary and Virginia Falls

This hike is a little further than our other easy trails but it is one of our most popular trails for hikers of all skill! There are many high points and places to really take in the scenery of this waterfall!

Trail of the Cedars

We wanted the park to be accessible to everyone no matter what, we build boardwalks alone to forest with many stopping points to rest with the family.

Hidden Lake

Hidden Lake trail is a moderate level trail. A trail renowned for its beatiful views and is only 2.8 miles.

Gunsight Lake

A moderate hike only taking 5 and a half hours. With stunning views of turquoise glacial watercolor.

Cracker Lake

A moderate trail with a lot of uphill climbing. With stunning views of Lake Sherburne and apline meadows.

Pitamakan Pass

Pitakaman Pass is a master level trail through the mountains with a once in a lifetime view of Lake McDonald

Ptarmigan Tunnel

This tunnel was sculpted in the 1930's and has a quarter of a mile hike through breathtaking caves

Swiftcurrent Pass

One of the most beautiful trails in our park, this trail is for experienced hikers only but has the most monumental views in the United States.

Pro-Tip #1
Be-ware of bears

Always bring your bear spray incase of any encounters with the wild life in our park

Pro-Tip #2
Arrive early

The park is very busy during the summer months, so it is best to arrive early to avoid the crowds and arriving early will allow you to stay out of the heat.

Pro-Tip #3
Weather Conditions

Double check the weather conditions before heading out on the trail. The weather can change quickly in the mountains.

Pro-Tip #4
First Aid

Always bring some type of first-aid incase of any injuries on the trail.

Park Fact #1
Park size

Glacier National Park is 1,583 square miles and is home to over 700 miles of hiking trails.

Park Fact #2
How many Glaciers?

In 1910 when it was named a national park, the park had 80 glaciers, today there are only 26 glaciers left.

Park Fact #3
Is it the G.O.A.T.?

The answer to that is up for interpretation but the symbol of the park itself is a mountain goat.

Park Fact #4
That's a lot of people...

The park has over 3 million visitors each year, making it one of the most visited national parks in the United States.
